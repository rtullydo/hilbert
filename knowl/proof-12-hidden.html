Let \((V, \norm{\cdot})\) be a finite-dimensional normed space. Suppose that \(K \subseteq V\) is closed and bounded. Then, since \(\rho(x) \leq \frac{1}{m} \norm{v}\) for all \(v \in V\text{,}\) \(K\) is also \(\rho\)-bounded. Because \(\norm{\cdot}\) and \(\rho\) are equivalent norms, they determine the same topology, and so \(K\) is also \(\rho\)-closed.
