Proof

Suppose that \(\hilbert\) contains a finite complete orthonormal sequence \(e_1, \ldots e_n\text{.}\) For any \(x \in \hilbert\text{,}\) find the error vector

\begin{equation*} y = x - \sum_{i=1}^n \ip{x}{e_i}e_i. \end{equation*}

Linearity of the inner product gives \(y \perp e_i\) for all \(i\) and so \(y=0\text{.}\) Thus \(e_1, \ldots, e_n\) is an orthonormal basis for \(\hilbert\text{.}\) Now define the operator \(U: \hilbert \to \C^n\) by

\begin{equation*} U(\la_1 e_1 + \la_2 e_2 + \ldots + \la_n e_n) = (\la_1, \ldots , \la_n). \end{equation*}

Then \(U\) is linear and bijective. From the fact that \(\norm{x}^2 = \sum_{i=1}^n \abs{\ip{x}{e_i}}^2\text{,}\) we infer that \(\norm{Ux} = \norm{x}\) for all \(x \in \hilbert.\) Thus \(U\) is a unitary operator, and so \(\hilbert\) is isomorphic to \(\C^n\text{.}\)

On the other hand, suppose that \(\hilbert\) contains a complete orthonormal sequence \((e_n)_{n \in \mathbb{N}}\text{.}\) Define the operator \(U: \hilbert \to \ell^2\) by \(Ux = (\la_n)_{n\in \mathbb{N}}\) where \(\la_n = \ip{x}{e_n}\text{.}\) By Theorem 5.4.3 (3), \(Ux \in \ell^2\) and moreover \(\norm{Ux} = \norm{x}\) for all \(x \in \hilbert\text{.}\) As \(U\) is defined in terms of the inner product, it is clearly linear. If \((\la_n) \in \ell^2\text{,}\) then by Theorem 5.2.3 the series \(\sum \la_n e_n\) converges to a point in \(\hilbert\) and by definition \(Ux = (\la_n)\text{.}\) Thus \(U\) is surjective. Hence \(U\) is a unitary operator, and so \(\hilbert\) is isomorphic to \(\ell^2\text{.}\)
