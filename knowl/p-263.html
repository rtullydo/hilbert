On the other hand, suppose that \(\hilbert\) contains a complete orthonormal sequence \((e_n)_{n \in \mathbb{N}}\text{.}\) Define the operator \(U: \hilbert \to \ell^2\) by \(Ux = (\la_n)_{n\in \mathbb{N}}\) where \(\la_n = \ip{x}{e_n}\text{.}\) By Theorem 5.4.3 (3), \(Ux \in \ell^2\) and moreover \(\norm{Ux} = \norm{x}\) for all \(x \in \hilbert\text{.}\) As \(U\) is defined in terms of the inner product, it is clearly linear. If \((\la_n) \in \ell^2\text{,}\) then by Theorem 5.2.3 the series \(\sum \la_n e_n\) converges to a point in \(\hilbert\) and by definition \(Ux = (\la_n)\text{.}\) Thus \(U\) is surjective. Hence \(U\) is a unitary operator, and so \(\hilbert\) is isomorphic to \(\ell^2\text{.}\)
