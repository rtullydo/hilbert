(A larger result, not proven here, is due to Frechet, von Neumann, and Jordan and states that in a normed space \((V, \norm{\cdot})\text{,}\) if the parallelogram law holds, then there is an inner product on \(V\) so that \(\norm{x} = \ip{x}{x}\text{.}\) We will see soon that while every inner product space carries a norm, the converse statement is not true. )
