For \(N \in \mathbb{N}\text{,}\) let \(y_N\) be the finite series \(y_N = \sum_{n=1}^N \ip{x}{e_n} e_n\text{.}\) Noting that \(d = \norm{x - y_N}\text{,}\) we can apply TheoremÂ 5.1.7 to get

\begin{equation*} \norm{x - y_N}^2 = \norm{x}^2 - \sum_{n=1}^N \abs{\ip{x}{e_n}}^2. \end{equation*}

Rearranging gives for all \(N\) that

\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=1}^N \abs{\ip{x}{e_n}} = \norm{x}^2 - \norm{x - y_N}^2 \leq \norm{x}^2. \end{equation*}

Thus in the limit as \(N \to \infty\) we have the result.
