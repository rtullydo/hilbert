Definition 2.1.1.

For a general complex vector space \(V\text{,}\) an inner product is a map \(\ip{\cdot}{\cdot}: V \times V \to \C\) satisfying the following properties for all \(x, y, z \in V\) and scalars \(c \in \C\text{:}\)

  1. \(\ip{x}{y} = \cc{\ip{y}{x}}\text{;}\)
  2. \(c\ip{x}{y} = \ip{cx}{y}\text{;}\)
  3. \(\ip{x + y}{z} = \ip{x,z} + \ip{y}{z}\text{;}\)
  4. \(\ip{x}{x} > 0\) if \(x \neq 0\text{.}\)

An inner product space is a pair consisting of a complex vector space \(V\) and an inner product \(\ip{\cdot}{\cdot}\) on \(V\text{.}\)

An inner product space is can also be called a “pre-Hilbert space”.
