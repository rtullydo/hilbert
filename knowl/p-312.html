Let \(\eps > 0\) and choose some \(N\) so that \(n, m \gt N\) implies that \(\norm{F_n - F_m} \lt \eps\) (as \(F_n\) is Cauchy). Then for any \(x \in V\) with \(\norm{x} \lt 1\text{,}\) and for all \(n, m \gt N\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \abs{F_m(x) - F_n(x)} \lt \eps. \end{equation*}

Taking \(m \to \infty\text{,}\) we get that, for all \(\norm{x}\lt 1\) and \(n > N\text{,}\)

\begin{equation} \abs{F(x) - F_n(x)} \leq \eps.\tag{6.1.1} \end{equation}

To complete the proof, note that since \(F - F_N\) and \(F_N\) are bounded on the closed unit ball of \(V\text{,}\) so must be \(F\text{,}\) which by TheoremÂ 6.1.7 means that \(F\) is continuous and so \(F \in V\ad\text{.}\) On the other hand, (6.1.1) implies that \(\norm{F_n - F} \lt \eps\) for \(n \gt N\text{,}\) and so \(F_n \to F\) in \(V\ad\text{.}\) Thus \(V\ad\) is complete (and hence a Banach space of continuous linear functionals).
