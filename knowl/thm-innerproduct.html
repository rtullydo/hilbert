Theorem 2.1.4.

Let \(V\) be an inner product space. For any \(x, y, z \in V\) and \(c \in \C\text{,}\)

  1. \(\ip{x}{y+z} = \ip{x}{y} + \ip{x}{z}\text{;}\)
  2. \(\ip{x}{c y} = \cc{c}\ip{x}{y};\)
  3. \(\ip{x}{0} = 0 = \ip{0}{x}\text{;}\)
  4. if \(\ip{x}{z} = \ip{y}{z}\) for all \(z \in V\text{,}\) then \(x = y\text{.}\)
