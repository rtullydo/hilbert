Chapter 7 Appendix: Results from analysis

Theorem 7.0.1. Heine-Borel.

A closed and bounded set in \(\R^n\) or \(\C^n\) is compact.

Theorem 7.0.2.

Let \(\mathcal X, \mathcal R\) be metric spaces. Let \(S \subseteq \mathcal X\text{.}\) If \(f: S \to \mathcal R\) is continuous, then \(S\) compact implies that \(f(S)\) is compact.

Theorem 7.0.3.

Suppose that \(K\) is a non-empty compact set in a metric space \(\mathcal X\) and that \(f:K \to \R\) is continuous. Then \(f\) attains its infimum and supremum on \(K\) - that is, \(f\) has both a minimum and maximum on \(K\text{.}\)
